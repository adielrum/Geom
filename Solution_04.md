### **Problem Statement**
Draw a diagram illustrating Theorem 9.

**Theorem 9**: Let $QRR'Q'$ be a trapezoid $(\overleftrightarrow{QQ}^{\prime}||\overleftrightarrow{RR}^{\prime})$. Let $l$ and $m$ be parallel lines through $Q$ and $R$. Let $l^{\prime}$ and $m'$ be parallel lines through $Q^{\prime}$ and $R^{\prime}$. Let $X=l\cap l^{\prime}$, $Y=m\cap m^{\prime}$, and $Z=\overleftrightarrow{QR}\cap\overleftrightarrow{Q^{\prime}R^{\prime}}$ . Then $X, Y$, and $Z$ are collinear.

*   Lines $l'$ and $m'$ are parallel (e.g., slanted).
*   The intersection points $X, Y$ and the intersection of non-parallel sides $Z$ align on a line.
